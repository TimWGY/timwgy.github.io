Projects

Human‑Centered Urban Digital Twin

Integrating multimodal data with LLM reasoning

Building city‑scale digital twins that combine imagery, text, mobility, and administrative data with LLM‑based reasoning to surface human‑interpretable drivers of urban experience and actionable planning signals.

Explainable Perception

Why places feel the way they do

A two‑stage GeoAI framework that parses free‑form explanations into structured perceptual units to quantify how specific elements shape safety, beauty, and liveliness across cities. ACSP 2025 presentation.

Street Connectivity

Public vs. private networks

National‑scale analysis separating regulatory impacts on public and private subdivisions with improved node classification and temporal trends for policy insight.

About & Contact

Bio

Hi, I am Tim Guangyu Wu, a PhD student at NYU Wagner and NYU Shanghai, researcher with the NYU Shanghai Urban Lab. My advisors are Professor ChengHe Guan and Professor Zhan Guo. I received my Master’s in Data Science from the Columbia Data Science Institute and my Bachelor’s in Data Science and Finance from NYU Shanghai. Previously, I worked at Columbia University’s Center for Spatial Research, where I led data science for the Mapping Historical New York project.

Interests

Human‑centered urban digital twins; GeoAI; multimodal data + LLMs; artificial general intelligence (AGI) for planning; and urban experience design.
