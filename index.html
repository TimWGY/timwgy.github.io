About

Hi, I’m Tim Guangyu Wu, a PhD researcher at NYU (Shanghai/Wagner). I study how people perceive cities and how data can guide better planning. My work combines city‑scale datasets, explainable GeoAI, and decision‑ready evidence to inform policy and design. I’m also building tools and collaborations that make urban analytics more transparent, reproducible, and practical.

Highlights

Explainable Perception

Why places feel safe or beautiful

A two‑stage framework that turns free‑form explanations into structured perceptual units to quantify how specific elements (e.g., leafy trees vs overgrown trees) shape perception across cities.

Connectivity

Public vs private street networks

National‑scale analysis that distinguishes regulatory impacts on public vs private subdivisions, with robust methods for node classification and temporal trends.

Parks & Green Space

Global park inventory

Integrating POIs and remote sensing to map and compare urban parks globally, enabling equitable access analysis and planning support.
